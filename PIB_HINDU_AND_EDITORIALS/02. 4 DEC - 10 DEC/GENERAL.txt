THE NATIONAL BIODIVERSITY AUTHORITY (NBA) - CHENNAI
- To Implement Biological Diversity Act (2002) (Statutory, Autonomous Body)
- Deals with conservation, sustainable use of biological resources and fair and equitable sharing of benefits arising out of the use of biological resources.
Objectives of the NBA And its Functions:
- intellectuat property rights on a research based upon biological research or knowledge obtained from India has to obtain prior approval of the NBA ( will impose benefit-sharing conditions )
- Ensures protection to the knowledge of local people relating to biodiversity through measures such as registration of such knowledge (Ex: http://www.thehindu.com/sci-tech/science/Tribe-offers-clues-to-hidden-wonders-of-medicinal-plant/article17195140.ece)
- advise the State Governments in the selection of areas of biodiversity importance to be notified as heritage sites and measures for the management of such heritage sites
Transfer of biological resource or knowledge 
- No person who has been granted approval, shall transfer any biological resource or knowledge associated to others except with the permission of the National Biodiversity Authority (NBA grants or rejects the proposal under public notification)
The State Biodiversity Boards (SBBs) AND BMCs
- The SSBs also regulate, by granting of approvals or otherwise requests for commerciai utilization or bio-survey and bio-utilization of any-biological resource by Indians.
- The local level Biodiversity Management Committees (BMCs) are respansible for promoting conservation, sustainable use and documentation of biological diversity and chronicling of knowledge relating to biological diversity

Hill Area Development Programme - (HADP) (pib_relid = 164421)
- The hilly areas of Manipur, Tripura and Assam have a distinct geo-physical entity and are lagging in socio-economic development.
- As a result of peculiar topography, there is a wide gap between the hill and valley districts in terms of infrastructure, quality of roads, health and education etc
- HADP was inspired with a serious research and deliberation of all these factors and is aimed at giving a focused attention to the lesser developed hilly areas
- To ensure equitable development of all the districts of the State, notwithstanding their diversities of topography, geographical conditions and other aspects.

Student READY programme:
ICAR Agricultural University has introduced an academic innovation “Rural Work Experience Programme” for the first time in the country during the year 1979-80. Based on its success, the programme was recommended by ICAR for adoption by all other Agricultural Universities in the country. This programme provided opportunities for final year students of B.Sc.(Ag.) to gain insight and experience in field operations and problems related to farming and rural life, by staying in villages for one semester. Now, ICAR has launched Students READY programme by integrating this “Rural Work Experience Programme” as a major component. The Students READY programme was recently launched by our Hon’ble Prime Minister on 25 July 2015 and it will be implemented from the next academic year. Under this programme, the Union Government shall provide fellowship of Rs 3000 per month for a period of six months to all the under-graduate students of agricultural science.

FINANCE COMMISSION
1. Article 280 in The Constitution Of India
- President shall constitute the finance commission (Once In Every Five Years)
- It shall consist of a Chairman and four other members to be appointed by the President
2. THE COMMISSION SHALL [ make recommendations to the President ]
- the distribution between the Union and the States of the net proceeds of taxes which are to be, or may be, divided between them 
- the allocation between the States of the respective shares of such proceeds
- the principles which should govern the grants in aid of the revenues of the States out of the Consolidated Fund of India [Article 275 in The Constitution Of India]
- any other matter referred to the Commission by the President in the interests of sound finance
3. The Commission shall determine their procedure and shall have such powers in the performance of their functions as Parliament may by law confer on them

NOTE:
1. The Chairman & its members can hold commission's office from the day of notification till the day they submit their report(on or before 2019).
2. 15th Finance Commission recommendations shall take effect for five years commencing on April 1, 2020.
3. Gazette Notification : http://www.egazette.nic.in/WriteReadData/2017/180483.pdf

U: http://pib.nic.in/newsite/PrintRelease.aspx?relid=174111
CATEGORY: FACTS-PRELIMS

Soil Health Card Scheme
-----------------------
1. SHC informs farmers about nutrients status of the soils
2. It also gives recommendation on appropriate dosage of nutrients to improve soil health and fertility
3. A farm will get the soil card once in every 2 years so that nutrients deficiency can be regularly detected and improved
4. This will reduce the cost of production and increase the output and income of the farmers

key features of Soil Health Card
---------------------------------
1. uniform approach to collect samples and test them in the laboratory
2. renew SHC every two years
3. GPS based soil sample collection has been made compulsory to monitor the changes in soil and to prepare a systematic database to compare them with the past years’ and such information is available in a GIS based Portal.
4. SHC portal has been linked to the Integrated Fertilizer Management System (iFMS) - And the distribution of fertilizers based on SHC's recommendation.

National Investment and Infrastructure Fund [ NIIF ]
----------------------------------------------------
OBJECTIVES
----------
To maximize economic impact mainly through infrastructure development in commercially viable projects, both greenfield and brownfield, including stalled projects

ABOUT
-----
- Headed by a Governing council for defining its future roadmap (Chaired by FM)
- This is different from National investment fund (Which is supposed to obtain the disinvestment proceeds).Later Govt. order made the disinvestment proceeds to go directly to the Public Fund of India.

[NHB is a principal agency to promote housing finance institutions both at local and regional levels]
- NIIF is envisaged as a fund of funds with the ability to make direct investments as required.
- NIIF can also invest in other SEBI registered funds [got registered with SEBI as Category II Alternative Investment Fund]
